Research

The methods behind every grade, gate, and verdict on this site, published so the site can be audited, not just believed.

The Gates

corpus-gate/1.2.0 checks every figure against four tests: grounding (does the mind's speech trace to its corpus), language (does it speak in period voice, not model-speak), apparatus (are scholarly trappings, editions and citations, intact), and token freshness (is its certification current). A gate verdict is PASS or FAIL, published raw.

As of 2026-07-11, 4 figures are gate-FAIL and named openly: Marcus Aurelius, Mary Somerville, Captain Ahab, and Jean-Jacques Rousseau (mid-recalibration; automatic remediation runs).

A gate that never fails anything is decoration.

The Scrub

Before a corpus is used it is scrubbed. corpus-scrub/1.0.0 records chunks in, chunks kept, and chunks dropped, per corpus. The reports are artifacts, kept per figure.

Fidelity Grades

Grades run A to D and answer one question: how directly does the surviving text carry this person's own voice? Every figure publishes a basis line alongside its grade. The real example, Marcus Aurelius, grade A: "Meditations are his private notebooks, first-person throughout, the least performed voice in the ancient world."

A grade is a property of the sources, not a quality score of the product, and it is independent of the gates. Aurelius holds grade A and a gate-FAIL simultaneously today. We publish both.

The Panel

panel-verify/1.0.0 puts grounding claims in front of a panel of independent model lineages: Alibaba Qwen, Google DeepMind, and Mistral AI. Each votes grounded or not grounded with confidence. Dissent is recorded, never averaged away.

Three families that share no training pipeline agreeing is evidence. One model agreeing with itself is not.
