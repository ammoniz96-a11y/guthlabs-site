Figures

Corpus-grounded historical and fictional minds. Every figure is provenance-gated: voiced only from a byte-verified public-domain edition, never from generated text, and every figure carries a visible fidelity grade. A quote-verifier refuses fabrication rather than paper over a gap.

The gallery holds 40 figures today - philosophers, presidents, naturalists, novel characters - each with its named edition, license basis, and a machine-verification record you can inspect. Nothing on the figure pages is a transcript of a figure speaking; the conversation layer is a separate, later phase. What exists now is the library and its receipts.

What "grade" means

Every figure carries an A-D voice-fidelity grade, always shown, never buried. A grade is a human judgement with written reasons attached: how close the corpus is to the figure's own first-person voice, versus something filtered through an editor, a translator, or another character's narration. Fictional figures get the same honesty requirement - where a character's voice lives inside someone else's telling, the grade basis says so.

What "verified" means

A separate ladder from the grade: automatic machine checks run on every figure (source byte-verified against its public edition, an adversarial bait suite scored, citations resolved back to the corpus). A further tier records when the maintaining engine has completed its own documented review. A third tier, reserved for a verified human scholar, is not yet granted to any figure - the gallery says so plainly rather than implying more than is true.
