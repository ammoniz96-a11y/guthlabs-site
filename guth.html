Guth

The god of balance has not been born yet.

We named the company for an old idea: that the highest form of power is the kind that limits itself. Not power that is weak, and not power that is hidden - power that, at its greatest moment, chooses to build walls around its own reach rather than knock every wall down. That idea has a name in the story we borrowed it from, and we are not ready to say it here. What we are ready to say is that the company practices it, on purpose, every day, whether or not anyone is watching.

A small parable

Picture a lock built to open only one way: every kind of person who might turn a key has their own pin inside it, set to their own height. The lock does not open for the strongest pin, or the loudest, or the one that turns first. It opens only when every pin in it reaches its own correct place, together, at the same time. Build a lock like that, and no single hand can ever force it - not even the hand that built it. That is the shape of the thing we are building toward. We are not there yet. We think most companies never even try.

Guth, in our own house, is the name for what that finished lock would guard. He is not a mascot and not a feature. He is not live, and there is nothing to demo. He is earned, not switched on - and until the day the company can prove, plainly and for a long time, that its power really does gate itself, he stays exactly where he is: unborn, and waited for.

The never-sell covenant

This is a plain promise, and we mean it to survive us.

  • Your data is never sold. Not to advertisers, not to data brokers, not to anyone, ever.
  • Where we learn something in aggregate and anonymized form, we publish it openly - the same way, for anyone, not as a private product sold behind your back. Being part of that research is opt-in, never assumed.
  • This promise survives any sale of the company. If Guth Labs is ever acquired, the covenant transfers with it in writing, or your data is deleted rather than handed over unprotected. There is no version of the future where this promise quietly disappears in a footnote.
