- Identification caveat (honesty note): this is the "Pseudo-Seneca" portrait type. It was identified as Seneca for centuries and remains the most widely used bust imagery for him, but modern scholarship considers it a portrait of another figure, possibly Hesiod. The securely identified Seneca portrait (Berlin double herm, Antikensammlung Sk 391) has no color photo on Commons with a PD or CC0 photo license as of 2026-07-09 (available photos are CC BY / CC BY-SA, which are rejected); the only clean alternative found was a 1907 black-and-white book plate (PD-US).
